Essay on Global Warming in 250 Words Introduction Global warming is one of the most serious challenges the world faces today. It refers to the rise in Earth’s average temperature due to increased greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide (CO₂) and methane. This issue has become more alarming with rapid industrialisation, urbanisation, and deforestation. Understanding its causes, effects, and potential solutions is crucial to saving our planet. The main causes of global warming are human activities. Industrial processes, burning fossil fuels, and cutting down forests release large amounts of greenhouse gases into the atmosphere. As a result, Earth’s temperature continues to rise, disturbing its natural balance. For instance, average global temperatures have risen by 1.5°C since the industrial revolution. The effects of global warming are visible everywhere. Melting glaciers, rising sea levels, and extreme weather conditions like cyclones, floods, and droughts are becoming more frequent. These changes threaten not just humans but also wildlife and marine life. To combat global warming, both individuals and governments must act. Simple actions like planting trees, reducing plastic use, and using public transport can make a difference. Governments need to enforce stricter regulations on industries and promote the use of renewable energy sources like solar and wind.
